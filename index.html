Learn: What is Projectile Motion?

Key Idea

A projectile is an object that moves through the air after being launched. If air resistance is ignored, the only force acting after launch is gravity.

Independence Principle: Horizontal and vertical motions are independent of each other, but they share the same time interval.

Core Vocabulary

trajectoryrangetime of flight maximum heightvix horizontal component viy vertical componentg = 9.8 m s⁻²

Equations

Horizontal: dx = vix t
Vertical: dy = viy t + ½at²
Vertical: vfy = viy + at
Vertical: vfy² = viy² + 2ady

Take a = −9.8 m s⁻² when upward is the positive direction.

Common Mistakes

❌ Never plug the total launch speed directly into horizontal or vertical equations. Always resolve into components first.
❌ The horizontal velocity does not change during flight (no air resistance). Do not subtract g from it.

Sign Convention

Choose upward as positive (+). Then:

• viy is positive for angled launches
• viy = 0 for horizontal launches
• a = −9.8 m s⁻² throughout
• vfy is negative at landing (object moving downward)

Problem-Solving Steps

  1. Define positive direction.
  2. Resolve vi into vix and viy.
  3. Use vertical motion to find time of flight T.
  4. Use dx = vix × T to find range.

Thinking Questions

For launches from ground level, which angle gives the greatest range?

Complementary angles (e.g. 30° and 60°) give equal ranges when launched from and landing at the same height. This is because sin(2θ) = sin(2(90°−θ)).
R = vi² sin(2θ) / g (ground-level only)
